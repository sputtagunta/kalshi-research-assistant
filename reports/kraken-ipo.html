Research Summary

Kraken, founded in 2011, is one of the largest cryptocurrency exchanges globally and has been considering going public for several years. The company has taken several steps that typically precede an IPO, including hiring investment banks, achieving significant revenue milestones, and expanding internationally. However, the crypto market’s volatility and regulatory uncertainty have created challenges for crypto companies pursuing public offerings. Kraken faces regulatory scrutiny in multiple jurisdictions and has had to adapt its business model accordingly. The company’s path to going public will likely depend on market conditions, regulatory clarity, and the overall health of the cryptocurrency sector between now and 2027.
